What is it?
This is a pool based upon the NFL season. Every week starting September 11, 2011 the NFL plays up to 16 games. The object of the pool is to correctly predict the winners of the games.

Who can play?
Pretty much anybody as long as you get permission through the pool administrator.

How much does it cost?
The cost of the pool is $1 per week for the weekly pool and $1 per week for the season pool. The season pool does have a $5 entry fee that is to be paid by the third week of the season; however, the fee covers the fee for weeks 13-17 of the pool. The weekly pool fee is to be paid any week that the player participates. The season pool fee is paid weekly to ensure the player's score gets added to his/her cumulative score. This includes any weeks that a season participant is unable to actually submit an entry. Both fees may be paid in full at the beginning of the season, and it is suggested that the player do so if he/she plans on participating every week for the full season.
Fee payments should be made in a timely manner to the pool treasurer. If fee payments are not up-to-date, the participant will receive zero scores until the necessary payments are made. The pool treasurer has the final verdict in these manners and will inform the pool administrator of problems if necessary. If a participant has a need to delay a payment, the pool treasurer is to be informed so no penalty will be assessed.

How do I participate?
Bookmark biskit.myzenhost.com in your browser. Every week, prior to kickoff, fill out an online form completely and hit submit. There are edits built in to help you get the form filled correctly. Then, if you haven't paid your money in advance, you need to give your fee to the pool treasurer. You can submit separate forms for weekly and season, or submit one form for both. You indicate on the form what pool you are playing. When you submit an online form, an automatic copy of the form contents is received by the pool administrator, the pool treasurer, and yourself to ensure there are no discrepancies. Please ensure the email address submitted on the form is correct and active.
The time listed on the email header will be the judge and jury determining if an entry has been submitted on time. If the administrator feels the entry was submitted too far after the kickoff, the player will automatically lose the games that are in progress when the entry was submitted.
If you are unable to get online to submit a form, paper forms and voice mail messages are considered valid entries. You may contact the pool administrator or treasurer with your entry.
Multiple entries for the same person are valid for both the weekly pool and the season pool. The only thing that is requested is that the season entries are named uniquely, ie. Joe 1, Joe 2. This allows the scores to be added to the correct totals each week.

How are scores calculated?
The scoring is based on a weighted point system. The first thing considered is whether the winner of each individual game is picked correctly. Then, each game during the week is given a point value starting with a maximum of 16. The player makes the decision as to which games he/she is most confident about and gives those the highest values. The games the player is most unsure about should be given the lowest point values. Each point total is to be used only once per sheet. When all the games for a given week have been completed, each participants point totals are computed. The participants score for the week is the sum of the weighted values for all the games he/she picked correctly. If there are less than 16 games played in a given week, the participants should discard the lowest point values when assigning points to games. The discarded point totals will be adding to the participants' scores automatically each week.
The season pool has the same basic rules, however, there is an additional rule to cover absentee entries. If a participant is unable to submit an entry during a week during the season pool, that person will receive a score equal to one point lower than the lowest submitted season entry score for that week.

When is a winner determined?
For the weekly version of the pool, the winner is the person with the highest point total.
The season pool winner is determined by the accumulated score of all the weekly entries into the season pool. The final winner is determined the day after the last game of the regular season in the NFL.

How much can I win?
The weekly winner of the pool receives the cash equivalent of all the weekly entry fees received. There are no runner-up prizes awarded in the weekly pool.
Depending upon number of entries received for the season pool, either the top three or top four accumulated scores will receive a portion of the total season fees received. The general breakdown of the prizes is approximately 70%, 20%, 10% for three payouts or 70%, 18%, 9%, 3% for four payouts. Payments for the season pool will be distributed in the days immediately following the last weekend of the NFL regular season.

What if there is a tie?
Ties are a possibility and are accounted for. There is a tiebreaker question on every entry that relates to a game or games that is being played in the NFL that week. In the event of a point tie, the tiebreaker question will determine the winner. If a tie remains after the tiebreaker question is evaluated, then the winner will be the person that picked the most games correctly. If a tie still exists, a coin flip will ultimately determine the winner.
The tiebreakers for the season pool are multiple tiered. If there is a points tie, then the participant with the most number of weeks with a greater score gets the tiebreaker. If a tie exists after that, the participant who picked the most game correctly over the course of the season gets the tiebreaker. If a tie still exists, then the participant with the highest inidividual week score will win the tiebreaker. In the event that the first three tiebreakers yield no winner, then a coin flip will be used as a last resort.
In the event there is a problem with the coin flip, a single-elimination javelin catching contest will determine the winner.

How will I know how I am doing in the pool?
Every week, return to the home page of the pool and click on the link to the standings page. There will be a list of every participant's score for the week, the standings for the season pool, and the list of the winners of the actual NFL games for the week. If events occur where the pool administrator is unable to update the standings for the week, alternate arrangements will be made to inform the participants of the scores.
